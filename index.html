A Zimbabwean architect builds for the "forgotten economy" by letting people design for themselves

When Zimbabwean architect Maxwell Mutanda embarked on a project to make life easier for street hawkers in his hometown of Harare, he discovered his guidance wasn't all that needed. "We thought, 'Okay let's design for them.' But as we entered that space we realized that they usually design for themselves," Mutanda says.
